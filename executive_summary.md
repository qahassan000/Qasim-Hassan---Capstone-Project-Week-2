# RetailGlobe Customer Segmentation
### Executive Summary — Prepared for the Board

---

## The Problem We Solved

RetailGlobe has been sending the same emails and promotions to every customer — from someone who spends thousands of pounds across dozens of orders, to someone who bought once two years ago and never returned. We analysed a full year of sales records (124,000 transactions, 3,994 customers) and divided our entire customer base into four distinct groups, each requiring a different strategy. The four segments are statistically robust, explaining 85% of the variation in how customers behave — these are real, meaningful distinctions, not guesswork.

---

## Top 3 Insights

1. **Two groups carry the business.** Our 147 Champions (3.7% of customers) and 876 Loyal Customers (21.9%) together generate 65.5% of total revenue. Champions buy frequently, spend the most, and purchased recently. Loyal Customers are the single largest revenue group at 45.4% — they are the commercial backbone, not Champions.

2. **The UK dominance is a volume problem, not a spending problem.** Nearly 88% of revenue comes from UK customers — but when we compared how much individual customers spend per order, international customers spend roughly the same (£250–£340) as UK customers. We do not need to change our product or pricing for international markets. We need more international customers.

3. **One in ten customers never came back.** 407 customers (10.2%) placed a single order and disappeared. A simple automated follow-up after a first purchase would recover a meaningful share of them at very low cost.

---

## Our Four Customer Groups

| Group | Size | Revenue Share | Profile |
|-------|------|--------------|---------|
| **Champions** | 147 (3.7%) | 20.1% | Buy often, recently, spend the most |
| **Loyal Customers** | 876 (21.9%) | 45.4% | Regular buyers, backbone of revenue |
| **At-Risk** | 2,440 (61.1%) | 32.5% | Drifting away, spending declining |
| **Dormant** | 531 (13.3%) | 2.0% | Have not engaged in months |

---

## 3 Recommendations for Emma

**1. Protect Champions personally.** Write to all 147 Champions this week — a personal message from Emma, not a bulk email. Thank them by name and give them early access to new products. The cost is negligible. Losing ten of them is not.

**2. Win back At-Risk customers before Christmas.** Send 2,440 At-Risk customers a personalised 10–15% discount in September, before the Q4 rush. Even a 5% response rate recovers tens of thousands of pounds at the most important trading time of year.

**3. Fix the first-purchase drop-off and grow internationally.** Every new customer should receive an automated follow-up two weeks after their first order. This directly addresses one-time buyer churn domestically and, given that international customers spend comparably to UK customers, the same approach applied to new international buyers is a direct path to reducing our dangerous dependence on a single market.

---

## What This Analysis Doesn't Tell Us

This tells us *what* customers have done, not *why*. We do not know why At-Risk customers stopped buying. We cannot distinguish between wholesale business buyers and individual consumers in the data — they require different strategies and a future analysis should separate them. The international spending finding is promising but is based on far fewer customers than the UK data, and should be validated before driving major investment decisions.
